AI Roof Inspection — Frequently Asked Questions

How does AI roof inspection work?

RoofCheck AI uses advanced computer vision to analyze photos of your roof. When you upload an image, the AI scans for visual indicators of damage — including missing or curling shingles, granule loss, moss growth, sagging areas, and signs of water damage. It combines image analysis with your answers about leak history and roof age to generate a roof health score from 1–10.

How accurate is an AI roof inspection?

Our AI is highly effective at identifying visible damage from photos, but it cannot fully replace a hands-on inspection. A licensed roofer can assess structural issues, underlayment condition, and flashing integrity that photos may not capture. We recommend using RoofCheck AI as a first step — if your score is 7 or below, book our free in-person inspection for a definitive assessment.

What roof problems can the AI detect from photos?

The AI can identify: missing, broken, or curling shingles; granule loss and bald spots on asphalt roofs; moss, algae, or lichen growth; cracking or blistering; visible sagging or structural deformation; damaged flashing around chimneys and vents; and general aging across tile, asphalt, metal, and flat roof systems.

Is the AI roof analysis really free?

Yes — 100% free with no hidden fees and no obligation to book any service. RoofCheck AI is offered as a public service for Los Angeles County homeowners to understand their roof condition before committing to repairs or replacement. Your emailed report is also free.

Do I still need a professional roofer after the AI inspection?

A score of 9–10 with no reported issues may only need routine annual maintenance. A score of 7 or below — especially with an active leak or visible damage — warrants a professional inspection. Our licensed team offers free in-person inspections across all of Los Angeles County. We give you an honest assessment with no pressure.
