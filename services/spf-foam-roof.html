Spray Polyurethane Foam (SPF) Roofing

SPF roofing is a seamless, monolithic system sprayed directly onto your roof deck. It delivers superior insulation (R-6+ per inch), self-flashes around every penetration, and — with proper coating maintenance — can last 30+ years. Palm Crest Roofing installs SPF on commercial, residential, and mixed-use buildings across LA County.

Key Benefits

  • Seamless, self-flashing — no seams to fail at penetrations
  • R-6+ per inch — the highest R-value per inch of any roofing insulation
  • Lightweight — typically under 1 lb per square foot
  • Installs over most existing roof types without tear-off
  • 30+ year service life with periodic coating recoats
  • Reflective elastomeric top coat for cool-roof performance

Our Process

Step 1: Inspection

Assess existing roof for moisture, structural issues, and substrate suitability.

Step 2: Prep & Repair

Clean the surface, repair blisters and punctures, ensure a sound substrate.

Step 3: Foam Application

Two-component polyurethane is spray-applied, expanding and curing within seconds into a seamless insulating layer.

Step 4: Elastomeric Coating

Reflective top coat is applied over the foam for UV protection, waterproofing, and reflectivity.

Step 5: Granule Broadcast

Optional silica or ceramic granule broadcast for walkability and additional UV resistance.

SPF Foam Roofing FAQs

How long does an SPF roof last?

The foam itself can last 30–50 years. The elastomeric coating needs to be recoated roughly every 10–15 years to maintain UV and waterproof performance.

Can SPF be installed over my existing roof?

Usually yes — SPF works over most existing roof substrates including built-up, modified bitumen, metal, and concrete, as long as the substrate is sound and dry.

Does SPF save energy?

Yes — significantly. SPF adds R-value and the reflective top coat reduces cooling loads. Many customers see 20–30% cooling cost reductions.
